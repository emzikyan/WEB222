History of Soccer

Soccer, also known as football, has a long and rich history that dates back thousands of years. Old Mesoamerican tribes produced the earliest recorded instances of team games utilizing a ball formed of a rock more than 3,000 years ago. Although there were several variations of the game played throughout a wide area, it was created by the Aztecs and was named Tchatali. The ball may represent the sun on certain ritual occasions, and the losing team's captain might be offered as a sacrifice to the gods. A rubber-made bouncing ball was a distinctive aspect of Mesoamerican forms of ball games; no other early civilization had access to rubber.

The first documented kick-and-ball game, known as cuju, was played in China during the third and second centuries BC. Cuju was played on a square-shaped space with a circular ball made of stitched leather with fur or feathers inside. Later, a modified version of this game known as kemari was played in ceremonial settings and spread to Japan.

Other types of ball games have existed since the time of Ancient Greece. The ball was created using hair-filled leather slivers. (the first documents of balls filled with air are from the 7th century). However, ball games were considered low status and were excluded from the Panhellenic Games. Games using balls did not take place during entertainment in the large arenas (amphitheaters) of Ancient Rome, but rather during military drills known as Harpastum. Football was introduced to the British Isles by the Romans. (Britannica). However, it is unclear to what extent the British population was inspired by this type and to what extent they had created their own variations.

In England, a game known as "mob football" was well-liked throughout the Middle Ages. Each team had a sizable number of people, and the game's ball was manufactured out of animal bladders. The goal was to get the ball to a certain spot, such a city gate or a cathedral. Early in the nineteenth century, English colleges and universities started to create their own versions of football. The rules varied significantly from location to location, but as several organizations started to develop, the game became more standardized.

The Football Association (FA) was founded in England in 1863 with the goal of developing a uniform set of regulations for the sport. These regulations established the corner kick, the penalty kick, and forbade the use of hands and arms (apart from by the custodian). The FA's regulations were swiftly embraced by other nations, and football started to take off globally.

Between Scotland and England, the first international soccer game was played in 1872. With the exception of 1942 and 1946 due to World War II, the World Cup has been staged every four years since its inaugural event in 1930.

Today, soccer is the most popular sport in the world, with an estimated 3.5 billion fans. It is played at all levels, from amateur to professional, and it continues to evolve as new strategies and techniques are developed.

Soccer Rules

  1. The game is played by two teams of 11 players each, including a goalkeeper.
  2. The objective of the game is to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal.
  3. The game is played in two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute break in between.
  4. The game starts with a kick-off from the center of the field. After a goal is scored, the game restarts with a kick-off from the center of the field by the team that conceded the goal.
  5. Players are not allowed to touch the ball with their hands or arms, except for the goalkeeper within the penalty area
  6. If a player commits a foul, the opposing team is awarded a free kick, penalty kick, or indirect free kick, depending on the severity of the foul.
  7. The game is stopped if the ball goes out of bounds, and is restarted with a throw-in from the team that did not touch the ball last before it went out of bounds.
  8. If the ball goes out of bounds over the end line, a corner kick is awarded to the opposing team.
  9. If the ball goes out of bounds over the side line, a throw-in is awarded to the opposing team.
  10. Offside rule: A player is in an offside position if they are closer to the opposing team's goal than the ball and the second-last defender, at the moment the ball is passed to them. If an offside player becomes involved in active play, the referee can award a free kick to the opposing team.
  11. Yellow cards and red cards can be issued by the referee for serious fouls or unsportsmanlike conduct. Two yellow cards will result in a red card, and the player must leave the field and their team plays with one fewer player for the rest of the game.
